14 thoughts on recovery:
1. There are three types of recovery:
• Physical recovery
• Mental recovery
• Spiritual recovery
2. You are either working or recovering on all three dimensions.
There is no in between.
3. You can operationalize recovery on three dimensions:
• Your mood
• Your energy
• Your clarity of thought
4. When you are well-recovered, you are in a good mood, with high energy, and a clear mind.
The opposite is also true.
5. When you are poorly-recovered, you are in a bad mood, with low energy, and a foggy mind.
6. There is no such thing as too much work, only too little recovery.
7. Good recovery activities restore your body, clear your mind, and make you excited about your regular activities again.
8. Some activities boost only certain areas, other activities boost all of them.
9. Some activities drain only certain areas, others drain all of them.
10. Alcohol boosts spiritual recovery at the expense of physical and mental.
Sometimes that’s worth it.
11. Adding close friends to any activity will boost your spiritual recovery with no downsides.
12. Leaving your phone behind for extended periods of time is the best mental recovery multiplier.
13. The activities that reliably boost all three for me:
• Hikes in nature with friends
• Full spa recovery sessions with my girlfriend
Both without my phone nearby.
14. Everyone will recover in different ways—your job is to find what works for you.

##post 2
7 more work mantras that have served me well:
1. Speed is king
Your goal as an entrepreneur is to:
• Build things faster
• Collect cash faster
• Answer questions faster
• Respond to people faster
This does not mean act recklessly.
But it does mean questioning the tempo & timeline of the way you currently do things yourself & within your organization.
2. Raise your standards
You are exactly where you are because you tolerate it.
Your employees perform exactly how they do because you tolerate it. 
Put simply, growth requires a raising of the minimum amount of performance you tolerate. 
3. Think bigger—no one else will
To raise your standards, you must have something that motivates you to put forth new discretionary effort.
This will fall into either:
• Family impact
• Social impact
• Customer impact
• Or material goods
You tolerate the current level of your business because (whether consciously or subconsciously) it is meeting the demands you’ve placed on it.
4. Profit, profit, profit
You cannot:
• Pay yourself with revenue
• Feed your family with revenue.
• Do anything of importance with revenue.
You must obsess over the profitability of your business.
5. Data, then decisions
Doing things is easy.
Figuring out the right things to do is hard. 
And it’s hard because you lack the information to make the right decision.
Which is why you need a tracking system that makes decisions for you.
So if you aren’t growing, it’s time to invest in data. 
6. Do the money math
Every single goal you have in this world is a simple set of equations.
And that’s especially true in business.
You should have crystal clear models of:
• Every marketing platform
• Every payroll allocation
• Every sales process
Each should be modeled well in advance so you know exactly toward what you are building.
7. Solve in sequence
The people who move fastest:
• Work on things in the right order
• At the expense of everything else they could work on
• For months and years on end
This is the discipline you are going to need.
